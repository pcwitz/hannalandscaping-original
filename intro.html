Project Guidelines

Target Audience

Homeowners vested in a neighborhood, interested in improving their whereabouts, their habitats. Our audience needs an outdoor space for leisure, for entertaining, for repose, with privacy. Our customers want to create a space with allure that gets people talking about what's behind that fence? A space that adds value, not only to their property as equity, but also in quality of life.

Purpose

I intend to present an irresistible welcome to Cincinnati homeowners looking for a landscaping company. I think of how many times I have disregarded a company because of the schlocky impression emanating from their website. This must be avoided.

My objective is to market the landscaping business. The site is acting as the premier portal to the outside, the first impression. It must demonstrate skills, experience, competence, and professionalism. Yeah, and it should help make money.

Furthermore, I'd like to include a dimension of customer service. I think this means being helpful. It should answer some basic questio…and be helpful.

The Big 10 Web Design Principles

  1. Be cognizant of the compatibility of fonts on different OS platforms. Some fonts are platform-specific e.g., Helvetica with Mac, and some are compatible with both, e.g., Arial.
  2. Be consistent in scale with margins, line-heights, and font sizes.
  3. Use an elastic layout and relative font sizes, measured in ems, when possible.
  4. Tweak text meaningfully. Experimenting is fine, but seldom an end in itself. If letter spacing adds clarity, then by all means; if it's because you always wanted to try it, then maybe think again.
  5. Be aware of white space and use it to achieve a clean design.
  6. Keep colors harmonious, and not too splendorous.
  7. Be sure the purpose of the site is clear.
  8. Test early, test often. Get to know your audience and design for them, not yourself.
  9. Check for appropriate contrast between text and background.
  10. Think about your content and whether is sufficiently uses keywords for search engine optimization.
  11. When all else fails, stick in the ol' trusty trowel.
